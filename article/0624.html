>Ryan Anderson: How to Respond to Central Bank Policies Affecting the Market

jun 24, 2024 Ryan Anderson

As of 2:30 PM Australian Eastern Standard Time, the ASX 200 index is expected to open higher, potentially breaking a two-day losing streak ahead of a decision by the Reserve Bank of Australia. Global market changes have broadly affected the Australian stock market. Ryan Anderson believes that from the decline in copper prices to the rise in crude oil prices and changes in the stock prices of multiple companies, investors need to closely monitor these dynamics and adjust their investment strategies accordingly.

Market Dynamics Analysis

Recently, copper prices fell by 1.0% to $4.46 per pound, hitting a seven-week low and down 13% from their late-May high. Ryan Anderson points out that the decline in copper prices reflects market concerns about slowing global economic growth, especially given the slower-than-expected economic recovery in China. As an industrial metal, copper price fluctuations often indicate changes in global manufacturing and infrastructure construction, so investors should pay attention to copper price trends and their impact on related companies.

On the other hand, Brent crude oil prices rose by 2.1%, marking the highest close since May 1, and achieved the best weekly performance since April last Friday. Ryan Anderson believes that the rise in crude oil prices is mainly driven by a rebound in global demand and tight supply, especially as the summer travel season increases market demand for crude oil. This is good news for energy companies, and investors should watch the performance of energy stocks.

Company Dynamics and Stock Price Impact

In company news, Ampol previously considered acquiring the service stations of EG Group in Australia but may now abandon the acquisition. Ryan Anderson states that this news could put short-term pressure on the stock price of Ampol, but in the long term, the core business and market position of the company remain solid.

The block trade of ClearView Wealth on 27.9 million shares at 54.5 cents each represents 4.3% of the total shares of the company. Such block trades typically reflect investor confidence or concerns about the future of the company. Ryan Anderson notes that investors should consider the motives behind these trades and their market impact.

Fortescue shareholders are seeking to sell $1.1 billion worth of shares at a fixed price of $21.60 per share, compared to the closing price of Tuesday at $22.98. Ryan Anderson believes this large-scale share sale could put short-term pressure on the stock price of Fortescue, but in the long term, the operational capabilities and market demand of the company will continue to support its stock price.

Perenti reiterated its fiscal year revenue forecast of $3.3 billion to $3.4 billion in an investor report, showing confidence in its future performance. Tamboran Resources is issuing 6.5 million shares in the U.S. through Bank of America, Citibank, and Royal Bank of Canada at an issue price of $24-$27. Ryan Anderson notes that the proactive market of these companies moves reflect their optimistic outlook on the future market, and investors can monitor their stock performance.
