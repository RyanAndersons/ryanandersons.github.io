Analysis: Exploring the Specific Impacts of CPI Data on the Stock Market

From Ryan Anderson's global perspective on the upcoming CPI data, he proposes several possible market response scenarios and analyzes the specific impact of each scenario on the stock market. Firstly, if the CPI increases by 0.15%-0.2%, Anderson expects this to strongly support a potential Fed rate cut, possibly driving the S&P 500 index up by 0.5%-1%. This market reaction reflects investors' optimism about moderate inflation and possible easing of monetary policy.

However, in scenarios where the CPI might increase to between 0.25%-0.3%, Anderson predicts a potentially negative initial market reaction, with the S&P 500 index possibly falling by 0.75%-1.25%. In such cases, rising housing-related inflation could intensify market concerns about the persistence of inflation, thereby affecting stock market performance. Anderson particularly notes that housing prices are a key factor affecting inflation stickiness, and their fluctuations significantly impact market trends.

On the other hand, if CPI growth is lower than expected, such as an increase of less than 0.1%, the market would likely view this as a positive signal, indicating easing inflation pressures. Under this scenario, Anderson predicts the S&P 500 index could rise by 1%-1.75%, as the market might expect the Fed to be more inclined to cut rates.
